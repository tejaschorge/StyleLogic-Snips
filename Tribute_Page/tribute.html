Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India | Former President

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, he played a pivotal role in India's cicilian space program and military missile development.

Achievements:

  • Played a crucial role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds".
  • Received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.
  • Advocated for education and youth empowerment, inspiring millions of students across the country.

Dr. Kalam inspired millions of people through his vision of a developed India and his emphasis on the importance of education, innovation, and integrity. He passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy continues to inspire future generations.
